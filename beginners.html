You Don't Need a Partner or Experience

Argentine tango is improvisational — there are no choreographed routines to memorize. Classes rotate partners, so come solo. The community is welcoming to everyone.

Getting Started
Beginners have several options! The easiest path is to check the calendar for the next class or guided practica. Most of our events are drop-in, meaning you can show up without advance registration for either members or non-members. A beginner-friendly class or guided practica is the ideal first step.
No — many people come alone and that's completely normal. Classes typically rotate partners so that leaders and followers both get the experience of dancing with different people. This helps you develop flexibility and adaptability rather than depending on one person's style.
Wear casual, comfortable, breathable clothing you can move freely in. For shoes, the key is being able to slide your foot along the floor and pivot easily. Flat leather-soled shoes or smooth-soled dress shoes work well. Avoid rubber-soled shoes (they grip and can torque your knees) and steel-toe boots. Women may eventually wear tango heels, but comfortable flats are recommended when starting out.
Most of our classes are drop-in, open to all levels including complete newcomers. Argentine tango focuses on technique and connection rather than memorized choreography, so instructors can introduce new concepts to the room even when there are experienced dancers present. You'll leave knowing something useful — a foundational concept or movement — that you can start practicing right away.
